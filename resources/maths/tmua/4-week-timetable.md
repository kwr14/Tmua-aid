# TMUA 4-Week Revision Timetable

This timetable turns the TMUA study pack into a day-by-day plan.

It assumes one main study block per day. If you have more time, add a short second block for error review or reattempts. If you have less time, keep the same order but reduce the number of questions.

## How To Use This Timetable

- Start each day by scanning the [One-Page Cheat Sheet](cheat-sheet.md) for `5 minutes`.
- Attempt the listed questions before reading the worked notes.
- End each day by recording:
- what topic the question was testing
- what the fastest method was
- what you missed first time
- Reattempt misses after `2 to 4 days`, not immediately.

Suggested session shape:

- `5 minutes`: quick review
- `30 to 45 minutes`: first question set
- `20 to 30 minutes`: second question set or corrections
- `10 to 15 minutes`: mistake log

- Review: [Cheat Sheet](cheat-sheet.md) trig section
- Goal: rewrite, factor, then count solutions carefully

### Day 5: Geometry And Circles

### Day 6: Sequences And Series

- Do: [Practice P1 Q4](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q4), [Practice P1 Q14](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q14), [2021 P1 Q3](worked/2021/paper-1.md#q3)

### Day 7: Review And Reattempt

- Reattempt: any `3 to 5` misses from Days 1 to 6
- Read: [Common Mistakes](common-mistakes.md)
- Goal: identify whether each miss was concept, recognition, or carelessness

## Week 2: Strengthen The Main Exam Themes

### Day 8: Calculus As Area And Shape

- Do: [Practice P1 Q5](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q5), [Practice P2 Q12](worked/practice/paper-2.md#q12)

- Goal: decide when area beats symbolic integration

### Day 9: Derivatives And Stationary Points

- Do: [Practice P1 Q18](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q18), [Practice P1 Q13](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q13)

- Goal: connect stationary points to increasing intervals and root counts

### Day 10: Geometry Reasoning

- Do: [2021 P2 Q7](worked/2021/paper-2.md#q7), [Practice P2 Q9](worked/practice/paper-2.md#q9)
- Review: [Common Mistakes](common-mistakes.md) section on similar triangles
- Goal: justify claims from diagram facts, not appearance

### Day 11: Circle Methods

- Do: [Practice P1 Q9](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q9), [2022 P1 Q14](worked/2022/paper-1.md#q14)
- Stretch: [2023 P1 Q17](worked/2023/paper-1.md#q17)

### Day 12: Number Structure And Logic

- Do: [2023 P2 Q4](worked/2023/paper-2.md#q4), [Practice P2 Q13](worked/practice/paper-2.md#q13), [Specimen P2 Q8](worked/specimen/paper-2.md#q8)

- Goal: use counterexamples and factor logic quickly

### Day 13: Trig Casework

- Do: [2021 P2 Q18](worked/2021/paper-2.md#q18), [Practice P1 Q10](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q10)
- Review: [Question Map](question-map.md) `Trigonometry`

### Day 14: Weekly Consolidation

- Make a shortlist of your weakest `2` topics
- Reattempt `2 to 3` questions from each weak topic
- Read: [Question Map](question-map.md)
- Goal: decide what needs extra attention before starting full-paper work

## Week 3: Mixed Sets And First Timed Papers

### Day 15: Mixed Topic Set A

- Do one mixed set of `8 to 10` questions chosen from:
- [Practice P1 Q2](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q2)
- [Practice P1 Q8](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q8)
- [2021 P2 Q2](worked/2021/paper-2.md#q2)
- [Practice P1 Q18](worked/practice/paper-1.md#q18)
- [2023 P2 Q4](worked/2023/paper-2.md#q4)
- Goal: identify the topic and method quickly

### Day 16: Timed Paper 1

- Sit: [Practice Paper 1](worked/practice/paper-1.md)
- Use the original paper PDF if you want a full simulation
- Goal: experience topic switching under pressure

### Day 17: Full Review Of Timed Paper 1

- Review every miss and every slow question from Day 16
- Tag each issue:
- `did not recognize topic`
- `used long method`
- `algebra slip`
- `interval/counting mistake`
- `careless reading`
- Goal: extract what cost time, not just what lost marks

### Day 18: Weak-Topic Repair

- Use [Question Map](question-map.md) to choose one weak algebra/graph topic and one weak geometry/logic topic
- Do `2 to 3` questions from each
- Goal: repair the exact gaps found in Timed Paper 1

### Day 19: Mixed Topic Set B

- Do one mixed set of `8 to 10` questions chosen from:
- [2021 P1 Q3](worked/2021/paper-1.md#q3)
- [Practice P2 Q9](worked/practice/paper-2.md#q9)
- [2017 P1 Q11](worked/2017/paper-1.md#q11)
- [Practice P2 Q12](worked/practice/paper-2.md#q12)
- [Specimen P2 Q4](worked/specimen/paper-2.md#q4)
- Goal: improve method recognition on unfamiliar-looking prompts

### Day 20: Timed Paper 2

- Sit: [Practice Paper 2](worked/practice/paper-2.md)
- Goal: apply lessons from the first timed paper

### Day 21: Full Review Of Timed Paper 2

- Review all errors and slow sections
- Compare with Day 17
- Goal: see whether the same mistakes are repeating

## Week 4: Exam Simulation And Final Polishing

### Day 22: Targeted Repair

- Pick your weakest topic from the two timed papers
- Use the `Best Starting Set` in [Question Map](question-map.md)
- Goal: get one unstable topic back under control

### Day 23: Timed Paper 3

- Sit: [2021 Paper 1](worked/2021/paper-1.md) or [2021 Paper 2](worked/2021/paper-2.md)
- Choose the one that best matches your weak areas
- Goal: move from practice papers into strong real-paper material

### Day 24: Review And Reattempt

- Review Day 23 deeply
- Reattempt the hardest `4 to 6` questions without notes
- Goal: convert review into actual retention

### Day 25: Final Mixed Topic Set

- Build a set of `10` questions from your own mistake log
- Include at least:
- `2` algebra/graph
- `2` trig/geometry
- `2` sequences/calculus
- `2` number/logic
- Goal: rehearse breadth and switching speed

### Day 26: Timed Paper 4

- Sit one of:
- [2023 Paper 1](worked/2023/paper-1.md)
- [2023 Paper 2](worked/2023/paper-2.md)
- [2022 Paper 1](worked/2022/paper-1.md)
- Goal: finish on a cleaner recent paper set

### Day 27: Final Review Day

- Read:
- [Cheat Sheet](cheat-sheet.md)
- [Common Mistakes](common-mistakes.md)
- your own mistake log
- Reattempt the `5` questions that still feel least secure
- Goal: consolidate, not cram

### Day 28: Light Confidence Day

- Do a short confidence set of `4 to 6` questions you now expect to get right
- Skim:
- [Conceptual Refresh](conceptual-refresh.md)
- [Question Map](question-map.md)
- [Cheat Sheet](cheat-sheet.md)
- Goal: finish calm, accurate, and method-focused

## If You Only Have 2 Weeks

- Merge Weeks 1 and 2 by doing two shorter topic blocks per day
- Keep Days `16`, `20`, `23`, and `26` as your timed-paper anchors
- Do not skip review days

## If You Are Already Strong

- Start from Week 3
- Use Weeks 1 and 2 only for targeted repair
- Increase the number of full timed papers and reduce note-reading time
